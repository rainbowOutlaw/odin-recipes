Moong Dal

Description

Moong dal is a wholesome vegeterian Indian meal that is glutton free and can be made vegan.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup moong dal (split and hulled mung lentils)
  • ⅓ cup onions finely chopped
  • ½ cup tomatoes chopped
  • 1 inch ginger finely chopped or minced
  • ¼ teaspoon red chili powder or ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • ⅓ teaspoon turmeric powder (ground turmeric)
  • 1.5 cups water for pressure cooking salt as required

Steps

  1. First rinse the moong lentils a couple of times in water. Drain the water and set the lentils aside.
  2. In a 3 litre stove-top pressure cooker take the rinsed lentils, chopped onions, chopped tomatoes and finely chopped ginger. Also add the turmeric powder, red chili powder and water to the cooker. Mix well.
  3. Pressure cook for 5 to 6 whistles on medium flame till the moong lentils are softened well. Once the pressure settles down, remove the lid and stir the dal.
  4. If the dal looks thick, then add some water and simmer for 1 to 2 minutes.
  5. Add salt. Mix very well and keep aside. Check the taste and if required you can add more salt.
  6. In a small pan, heat oil or ghee or butter. First fry the cumin seeds.
  7. Next add the garlic and green chili and fry for some seconds. Don't brown the garlic. Switch off the flame.
  8. Now add the garam masala powder, red chili powder and asafoetida. Switching off the flame earlier ensures that the spice powders don't get burned.
  9. Quickly stir and immediately pour the tempering mixture in the dal.
  10. Stir the dal and serve hot moong dal with steamed rice or chapatis.
  11. The moong dal tadka tastes better as it is and there is no need to garnish or add coriander leaves to it.
  12. But if you want you can always garnish with some coriander leaves. For a slight tang you can also squeeze some lemon juice.
